How Normal Copy Traders Function Today

Traditional copy trading platforms rely on human traders to make decisions. Here’s how they typically work:

1. Manual Selection of Traders

Users manually select traders to copy based on their past performance, risk appetite, and trading style. This process can be time-consuming and often relies on incomplete or outdated information.

2. Delayed Execution

Once a trader is selected, there is often a delay between the trader’s actions and the copying of trades. This delay can result in missed opportunities or less favorable entry/exit points.

3. Limited Transparency

Traditional copy trading platforms often lack transparency in how trades are executed. Users may not fully understand the strategies being used or the risks involved.

4. Emotional Bias

Human traders are prone to emotional decision-making, which can lead to inconsistent performance and increased risk.
